## LT Process for Escalating a Customer Issue to Support Incident Coordination for resolution

1. Join the Slack #lt-direct-escalations channel 
2. Share a brief description of why the customer escalated their issue to you. Please include Zendesk ticket #. If Customer does not have one, they may be referred to https://support.github.com/
3. If this is URGENT ie needs close to real time engagement please run .pager trigger support-incident-coordination (LT Escalation) in the #lt-direct-escalations Slack channel 
4. If you require updates beyond acknowledgement, please add 'update me'

IC will acknowledge your escalation 
IC will then review the Zendesk ticket to determine current status and 
engage with the Supportocat that is assigned to work the ticket to determine if any assistance or additional technical resource is needed
IC  will provide an initial status update including next steps and will provide ongoing updates in the #lt-direct-escalation Slack channel until resolution
In the event that the customer’s GitHub environment is down or in a significant degraded state, then the full incident escalation process will be initiated:
Creation of dedicated Slack for supportocats to collectively troubleshoot the issue
Additional technical resources will be brought in to assist as needed
Creation of conf bridge for GitHub technical resources to join and collectively troubleshoot
Updates will be provided every 30 min via the #lt-direct-escalation 
For visibility of all open SIC engagements, please use the SIC Dashboard
